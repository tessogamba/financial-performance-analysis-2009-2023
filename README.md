# Financial Performance & Valuation Insights (2009–2023)

**Author:** Tess Ogamba  
**Tech:** Power BI Desktop (DAX, Power Query), Star Schema, GitHub  
**Dataset:** Financial statements of major companies(2009–2023)

---

## Executive Summary

This project analyzes 15 years of company financials like Apple, Microsoft, Google, Amazon, NVIDIA, Intel, etc., to answer investor questions like:  
- Who grew the fastest and most consistently?  
- Who is most profitable and efficient?  
- How do companies compare across industries?  
- What does long-term (CAGR) growth look like?  
- Which companies show financial risk (e.g., leverage)?

I built a Power BI semantic model (star schema) and created DAX measures for revenue, profitability, efficiency, leverage, and growth. The result is an interactive dashboard suitable for investor updates, equity research, or executive reviews.

---

## Business Purpose

Executives and analysts need a compact, reliable way to:
- Track performance trends across years,
- Benchmark peers within and across industries,
- Identify leaders/laggards on profitability and growth,
- Spot risk, e.g., debt levels and resilience (cash flows).

This dashboard turns raw financial statements into decision-ready KPIs.

---

## Key Questions

1. Which companies exhibit sustained revenue growth (YoY and CAGR)?
2. Which categories/industries (IT, banking, finTech, manufacturing, food/retail) show higher margins?
3. How do profitability (gross margin, net Margin) and cash generation interact?
4. What are the returns on capital (ROE, ROA)?  
5. Where does leverage (Debt/Equity) increase risk?
6. Which companies look strongest on a balanced score (growth + margins + cash)?

## Data Description

- **Year** — calendar year of results.  
- **Company** — company ticker/name, e.g., AAPL, MSFT, GOOG, etc.  
- **Category** — industry group, e.g., IT, Bank, FinTech, Manufacturing, Food.  
- **Revenue** — total money from selling goods/services.  
- **Gross Profit** — Revenue minus direct costs to make/provide the product (materials, manufacturing, etc.).  
- **Net Income** — final profit after all costs, interest, and taxes.  
- **EBITDA** — operating earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, amortization; a proxy for operating cash.  
- **Shareholder Equity** — owners’ net value in the company (Assets − Liabilities).  
- **Cash Flow from Operating** — cash generated by the core business this year.  
- **Cash Flow from Investing** — cash used for or received from investments, e.g., capex, acquisitions.  
- **Cash Flow from Financial Activities** — cash from borrowing/repaying debt, issuing/buying shares, paying dividends.  
- **Earning Per Share (EPS)** — profit *per share* of stock.  
- **Current Ratio** — short-term liquidity (current assets ÷ current liabilities).  
- **Debt/Equity Ratio** — leverage (debt ÷ equity).  
- **ROE / ROA / ROI** — returns generated relative to equity / assets / investment.  
- **Net Profit Margin** — Net Income ÷ Revenue.  
- **Free Cash Flow per Share** — (Operating Cash − Capital Expenditure) per share (here given directly).  
- **Inflation Rate (US)** — background macro context for the year.

## Modeling (Star Schema)

**Fact table**
- `fact_financials` — one row per company per year with numeric metrics.

**Dimensions**
- `dim_Date` — Year (and a simple Date field for time functions).
- `dim_Company` — unique list of companies.
- `dim_Category` — unique list of industries.

**Relationships**
- `dim_Date[Year]` → `fact_financials[Year]`  
- `dim_Company[Company]` → `fact_financials[Company]`  
- `dim_Category[Category]` → `fact_financials[Category]`

---

## Core DAX Measures

**Scale**
- `Total Revenue = SUM(fact_financials[Revenue])`  
- `Total Net Income = SUM(fact_financials[Net Income])`  
- `Total Gross Profit = SUM(fact_financials[Gross Profit])`  
- `EBITDA Total = SUM(fact_financials[EBITDA])`  
- `Number of Companies = DISTINCTCOUNT(fact_financials[Company])`

**Profitability**
- `Gross Margin % = DIVIDE([Total Gross Profit], [Total Revenue])`  
- `Net Profit Margin % = DIVIDE([Total Net Income], [Total Revenue])`

**Growth**
- `Revenue YoY %` = current year vs. prior year using `DATEADD(-1, YEAR)`  
- `Net Income YoY %` = same idea for net income  
- `Revenue CAGR %` = long-run compound growth between min/max selected years

> Additional advanced measures (to be added next): ROE, ROA, EBITDA Margin, Debt/Equity, Current Ratio, Cash Flow ratios, Composite Health Score.

## Report Design (Overview)

**Page 1 — Executive Overview**
- Slicers: Year, Company, Category  
- Cards: Total Revenue, Total Net Income, Gross Margin %, Net Margin %, Revenue YoY %, Revenue CAGR %  
- Line: Revenue by Year (category legend)  
- Column: Net Income by Company (selected year)

**Page 2 — Profitability & Efficiency**
- Gross Margin %, Net Margin % by Company  
- EBITDA Margin by Company  
- Scatter: Margin vs. Revenue Growth (quadrants)

**Page 3 — Cash & Leverage**
- Operating Cash Flow trend  
- Debt/Equity by Company  
- Current Ratio by Company  
- Waterfall: Cash movement (Operating, Investing, Financing)

**Page 4 — Company Lens**
- Company selection → mini profile (KPIs + trends + narrative notes)

## Methodology Notes

- Power Query used for cleaning (trim headers, set data types, split dimensions).  
- DAX used for business logic (time intelligence, margins, growth).  
- Star schema keeps calculations simple, fast, and reliable.  
- Slicers drive context (each measure recomputes under filter selections).

## How to Reproduce

1. Clone or download this repository.  
2. Place the CSV in `./data/` and open the PBIX in `./pbix/`.  
3. (Optional) Re-run Power Query steps to confirm data types and dimensions.  
4. Use the slicers to explore insights.

---

## Limitations & Assumptions

- CSV is assumed clean and annual (no quarterly breakdown).  
- Ratios supplied by the dataset are taken at face value.  
- Inflation is contextual; no CPI deflation of nominal values applied (future enhancement).  
- A daily date table was not required; analysis is annual.
